What do you really know about your energy usage?

Due to the movement from our family home to our first own shared flat at the beginning of our studies we quickly realised how less we actually know about housekeeping and particularly electricity.

• How much does one kilowatt hour cost?
• What can I actually do with it?
• What does "watt" actually mean?
• Which devices have the highest consumption?

Our goal of this project was to arouse consciousness for electricity consumption in our every day life and as a result of that to lead to sustainable behaviour and usage.

01 Empathy

Process

After interviewing our fellow students, we realized that we are not alone with our lack of knowledge. Especially students, who rent a shared flat with a fixed price contract and due to that do not have to deal with those topics felt caught by our questions.

Top insight

This lack of knowledge unfortunately leads to unsustainable behaviour with energy since the basic knowledge to act responsible is missing.

02 Research

Energy consumption in our daily life

To answer our previous stated question, we did a little research on the numbers in regards to our daily energy usage. In average, every one of use needs 6 kilowatt hours in a day.

03 Ideation

Process

The goal of our product was to lead the users to lower their everyday energy consumption and higher their own energy expenditure. Due to that it is constituted of 2 components:
1. Tracking of the energy usage at home
2. Gamification app

Energy tracking

We built a device to track the daily usage at home and then visualize that data in our app. With that the users have an overview and learn about their devices.

Gamification app

The app then displays the data. But our goal was not only to raise the consciousness, but also to sensitize for the efford which stands behind that. We want to answer the question: How much do I have to walk, to generate enough energy to charge my phone? Since one step equals 0,057Wh and most of our samples walk 3000 steps on a day in average, they currently already generate four hours of their laptop running time every day. Therefore in our gamification the user can choose a device as their opponent and beat them with generating more energy then they were using until the end of the day.

04 Live Wireframes

Design

The design of our app is colorful and playful. We wanted to use the concept of gamification to convey our message.

Main screen

The main screen shows how many steps the user already walked on that day and how many steps are missing, to beat their opponent-device (laptop).

Tracking and data visualization

To visualize the data we got inspired by the Apple Health App. The user should have the opportunity to witness his progress over time. The percent value always refers to the walked steps and displays the success of that day.

05 Coding

For the technical realization we read off the junction box with a infrared sensor and sent those data via HTTP request and nodejs to a PHP database. To read off the data from the single devices, we attached CT-sensors to them. We first built our own step sensor but then quickly realised that we also can access the data which our phones already generate. We coded our app as a web-app with HTML/CSS and Javascript.
